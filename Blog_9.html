The Global Climate Policy and Agreements

BY Youth Climate Connect

11 MIN READ

Global climate policy and agreements, notably exemplified by the Paris Agreement, continue to be a central focus in international discussions on climate change. The Paris Agreement, adopted in 2015 under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC), represents a landmark global commitment to combatting climate change. One of the key aspects of the Paris Agreement is the goal to limit global temperature rise to well below 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels, with efforts to limit it to 1.5 degrees Celsius. This ambitious target underscores the urgency of reducing greenhouse gas emissions and transitioning to a low-carbon economy to mitigate the impacts of climate change.

Countries participating in the Paris Agreement have submitted nationally determined contributions (NDCs), outlining their individual targets and plans to reduce emissions and enhance climate resilience. These NDCs serve as the cornerstone of global climate action, reflecting each country's commitment to addressing climate change based on its national circumstances, capabilities, and priorities. The collective efforts of countries to implement their NDCs are crucial for achieving the overarching goals of the Paris Agreement and advancing towards a sustainable and climate-resilient future.

However, challenges persist in global climate cooperation, including the need for increased ambition in emission reduction targets, financing climate mitigation and adaptation efforts, technology transfer, capacity-building, and addressing climate justice and equity concerns. Bridging these gaps requires strengthened international cooperation, political will, financial commitments, and innovative solutions to accelerate climate action and meet the goals set forth in the Paris Agreement.

The ongoing dialogue and negotiations within the UNFCCC framework, including COP meetings and subsidiary bodies, play a vital role in advancing global climate policy and agreements. These platforms provide opportunities for countries, stakeholders, and non-state actors to collaborate, share best practices, exchange knowledge, and mobilize resources for climate action. Additionally, initiatives such as the Green Climate Fund (GCF) and international partnerships on climate finance facilitate the mobilization of financial resources to support developing countries in their climate mitigation and adaptation efforts, fostering a more inclusive and equitable approach to addressing climate change on a global scale.

Additionally, the Paris Agreement framework emphasizes the principle of common but differentiated responsibilities and respective capabilities (CBDR-RC), recognizing the varying levels of development, historical emissions, and capacities among countries. This principle underscores the importance of ensuring that climate actions are fair, transparent, and inclusive, taking into account the unique circumstances and needs of both developed and developing nations. Furthermore, the Paris Agreement encourages enhanced ambition over time through the process of ratcheting up NDCs, wherein countries are expected to periodically revise and strengthen their commitments to align with the long-term goal of achieving net-zero emissions by mid-century. This iterative process of ambition-raising fosters continuous progress in global climate action and reinforces the collaborative spirit of international climate cooperation under the Paris Agreement framework.

In conclusion, global climate policy and agreements, epitomized by the Paris Agreement, are essential instruments for addressing the complex challenges of climate change. While progress has been made through international cooperation and commitments, continued efforts are needed to ramp up climate ambition, enhance collaboration, mobilize resources, and implement transformative actions that align with the objectives of the Paris Agreement and contribute to a sustainable and resilient future for all.
